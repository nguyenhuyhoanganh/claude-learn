Similarly, if the developer team, if they are trying to analyze a production issue, they can try to understand what are the sequence of events happen behind the scenes, and accordingly, they can try to initiate the same kind of actions on another new account. So there are many, many, many use cases that are supported by the event sourcing. That's why many of the big organizations like e-commerce applications, OTT applications, social applications, they are going to leverage event sourcing because if they store all the events that are being triggered by the end user. They can take this data, they can perform analysis, they can take informed decisions. In case if an UI application is looking only for the most current data, then they always welcome to query the read database because that information is readily available on the read database. Apart from this, there are many advantages you are going to get since you have two separate database on the write and read side. Now you have complete freedom on choosing the databases based upon your use cases you can do the independent scaling and when the write database is down, there won't be any impact onto the read side.
