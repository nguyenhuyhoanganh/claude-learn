# Common Transformers

(cheerful piano music) Instructor: Now let's take a look at how we can use Kustomize to modify or transform our Kubernetes configs. And the way we do this is through something called Kustomize Transformers. And Kustomize has several different transformers built in. You can even create your own custom transformers. But in this lecture, we're gonna focus on a specific subgroup of transformers called Common Transformations. And before we even go over what that is and what they do, let's take a look at the issue they're trying to address first. In this example, we have our deployment.yaml file and a service.yaml file. So pretty straightforward. And let's say that we want to apply some common configuration across all of our yaml files. So we want all of our Kubernetes resources to have some common configuration. Well, let's say that we want to specifically add a label in this case, so something like org KodeKloud, or maybe we wanna go into all of our Kubernetes objects and add a specific prefix or suffix to the name. So maybe we wanna append the word -dev to the end of our name, and we'll do the same thing across all of our Kubernetes configs. Now, we can go into each one of our yaml files and add these in. But keep in mind, in a production environment, you're going to have significantly more than just two yaml files. And so doing this by hand is not a scalable solution, and it's gonna be very time-consuming and it's going to lead to a lot of errors. And so this is why we have these common transformations in Kustomize. What they do is they allow us to make these common configuration changes across all of our Kubernetes resources. So let's go over some of the common transformations that we can apply. So we have the commonLabel transformation, and we just kind of saw what that does. That basically just adds a common label to all of your Kubernetes resources. We also have the namePrefix and Suffix transformation. This is going to allow you to add a prefix or suffix to the names of all of your Kubernetes resources. We've also got one for Namespace, so you can put all of your Kubernetes resources under a specific namespace. And we also have commonAnnotations. So if you wanna add some sort of metadata, some annotation to all of your Kubernetes resources, you can use the commonAnnotation transformation to apply it across the board. So let's take a look at these transformers in action. We'll start off with the commonLabel one, it's the simplest one. So let's say we wanna add a label to all of our Kubernetes resources. Well, we would go into our Kustomization.yaml file, and just add the commonLabel property and then specify the labels that you wanna add, and that's gonna go ahead and add this to all of your Kubernetes resources. Or more specifically, it's going to add it to all of the resources that are being imported by this specific Kustomization.yaml file. Now let's take a look at the Namespace Transformer. So the Namespace Transformer is just going to put all of your Kubernetes resources under a specific namespace. To do that, it's very simple. You just do namespace, then the name of the namespace, and then that's going to get applied to all of your Kubernetes resources. For the Prefix/Suffix Transformer, we can add in a specific prefix or suffix by providing the name prefix or the name suffix properties. And that's gonna map to the prefix of the name as well as the suffix of the name as well. And for the last common transformation, we have the annotation transformation. So if you wanna add a specific annotation to all of your Kubernetes resources, you could just specify commonAnnotations in the Kustomization.yaml file, and then specify your desired annotation. And that's going to get added to all of your Kubernetes objects.
