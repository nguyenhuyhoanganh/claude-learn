# Terminating cloud services

As we approach the end of the course, I want to warn you about potential unexpected costs that can arise from using cloud services. While rare, these situations can happen, and I really don't want you to be one of those cases that ends up in online discussion forums or in the media with unexpectedly high bills. So I just added here an example with Netlify. So this is a user, and this is a bill that they got for just simply hosting a static website. Now that I have your attention, let's discuss what you need to do to avoid such situations. Now, when you registered for Netlify, it was indeed a plan that was free. So if you're taking a look here at the plans, you can see here the starter plan and you'll see here $0. If you actually read the fine print, it's $0 to get started and then pay as you go. Now, for everything that we did in this course, you shouldn't really have any additional cost because the usage was absolutely minimal. But still, if we scroll here further down and understand what the starter plan includes, we're going to see here that actually there are additional costs. For example, for the bandwidth you can pay $55 for 100GB. And here further down you can find also additional examples for other costs that can be countered. So to avoid any of these costs listed here, remove any website you have created on Netlify. And if you're still learning and experimenting with Jenkins and Netlify and just need a bit more time, that's absolutely fine. Simply set a reminder in your calendar for, let's say, one month from now and come back then and terminate any projects that you have in Netlify. That should be enough time to learn how to use this. The same cautionary tale applies to AWS as well. So here is just an example from someone who got a $20,000 bill from AWS, and you can see if you're searching online, there are many examples of this. So the moral of the story is to terminate any AWS services you have created to avoid unexpected costs. Right now you may be on a free plan, but that free plan is over after 12 months. So you may even forget about AWS. And one year from now you could get a bill from AWS from some services that are still running on your account without you actually getting any benefit. So just set a reminder in your calendar to check your AWS bill after one month to ensure that there's nothing still running. Even if you thought you removed everything that you have used in AWS. And if you do receive an unexpected bill from AWS, please send me a message. While I cannot promise anything. It might be able to help you with this, but let's not get there. Follow my advice and terminate any service and any resources and any cloud resources that you have been using throughout this course.
