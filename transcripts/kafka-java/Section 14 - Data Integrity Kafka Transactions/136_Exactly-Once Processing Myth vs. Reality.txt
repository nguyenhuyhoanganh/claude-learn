# Exactly-Once Processing: Myth vs. Reality

This will be a quick lecture and I want to clear up a very common misunderstanding, the idea of Kafka's exactly once processing. We can see the Kafka team often saying this as part of their presentations in their website etc. Kafka provides exactly once processing, we give that guarantee etc. So that's how they will talk. They are not wrong technically, but sometimes it causes some misunderstanding. When Kafka talks about exactly once semantics, it's speaking only from the Kafka's point of view. Kafka can guarantee that consuming the request, producing the output messages and acknowledging the request etc. happen atomically within Kafka and that's it. But in the real life, when you have microservices architecture, it is not like you have only Kafka. So you will be having your database and you will have other microservices that might depend on other databases and Kafka has no visibility into your application logic. Within your processor application, you might be inserting updating records into your database or you might be calling another microservice which could be updating its database and so on. So Kafka has no idea about all those things. So when the transaction fails, spring will retry. So you might be inserting records into your database again or you might be calling this remote service again, which in turn could be inserting its database again. You are getting right. So your application as a whole is not automatically exactly once processing. As developers, we have to be careful here. Kafka transactions provide exactly once guarantee only at the messaging layer, not across databases, HTTP calls or third party systems. Please remember that this is our problem. We have to handle this in our application.
