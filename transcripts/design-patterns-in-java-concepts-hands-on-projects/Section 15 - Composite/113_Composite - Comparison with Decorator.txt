# Composite - Comparison with Decorator

Now let us compare our composite design pattern with decorator. So a composite deals with a tree structure of objects. So our composite class will have multiple children within it. A decorator, on the other hand, deals with a single object which is contained within itself. It doesn't have this concept of children. Now, leaf nodes and composites, they have the same interface. However, composites only delegate the operation to their children. Decorators, on the other hand, are supposed to add or modify the behavior that is contained within them. Okay, so that is the whole purpose of a decorator. So decorator will always add something or change something from the behavior of the original object. Now, decorator, of course, doesn't have the notion of children, which the composites have. All right. So this is the difference between the composite design pattern and decorator design pattern.
