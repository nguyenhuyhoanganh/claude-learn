# Chain of Responsibility - Pitfalls

Now let's look at some of the pitfalls of chain of responsibility. The first pitfall is that there is no guarantee provided in the design pattern that tells you that your request will be handled in this chain of responsibility. In fact, the design pattern explicitly states that there is a possibility that your request will traverse the entire chain and fall off at the other end. And you would never know what happened to your request, whether some action was performed or not. Okay, so you should be aware of this limitation of this design pattern when using chain of responsibility. Now, next fall is actually not part of the design pattern or not fault of the design pattern, but the way we create chains, there is always a chance that we can misconfigure our handler chain and that means we may leave off some of the objects that we intended to add into our handler chain. Okay. Or you could add same object twice in two different places in your chain. Okay, so the code which creates this chain should be reviewed and it should be unit tested to make sure that we are not leaving or we are not making unwanted connections in our handler chain.
