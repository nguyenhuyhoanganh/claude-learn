# Section 5 Intro

Here is the section where things get really interesting. Elastic search isn't just research, it has powerful aggregation capabilities too. So if you're indexing structured data, like weblogs for example, elastic search and the elastic stack can be used to quickly analyze and even visualize that data. Without much work, you can create something that looks a lot like Google analytics that can scale up to even the largest web sites, and that's just one example of what you can do. What's really exciting is that elastic search can aggregate massive data sets in just a matter of milliseconds, so for many common operations, elastic search can be a much faster alternative to things like Hadoop or even Spark and Flink... Imagine getting an answer for something like the ninety ninth percentile of page rendering times on your web site over the past month, in under a second; when an equivalent job on Hadoop might take hours to complete. You can see why elastic search is a very important tool to know about. As always, I'll teach you about aggregations in elastic search using real hands on examples. Let's dive in and see what elastic search can do.
