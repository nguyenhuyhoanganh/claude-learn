Elasticsearch is not just for search anymore. You can actually use an Elasticsearch cluster as a very efficient way of analyzing your structured and numerical data that might be living in an Elasticsearch index. And you can compute metrics on that data very quickly, automatically and very easily. You can do things like compute the average of a given field. Get stats about their percentile distributions of the values in those fields. Get the minimum and maximum values and stuff like that very easily. You can also bucket your data very quickly and efficiently as well. This is very useful for things like histograms, where you need a group data together based on certain ranges or even distances in geo targeting or geofencing. You can also do things with significant terms for things like text analysis, and you can do even more complicated things by putting these things together in pipelines that lets you do things like moving averages and getting the averages of different buckets or doing cumulative sums over time. There are also features in here for dealing with matrices. So if your data is two dimensional or higher in nature, Elasticsearch can handle that now as well. What's really cool is that a lot of the stuff that you can use Elasticsearch aggregations for can sometimes take the place of what you would do before using Hadoop or Spark on a giant cluster. The difference is that if you try to analyze a huge dataset on a Hadoop cluster or even using Spark, which is even faster than Hadoop, the results still are not going to come back instantaneously. There's still a lot of overhead and actually kicking off a Hadoop or a spark job and collecting all the results back together. So in many cases, you can get an answer back in milliseconds with Elasticsearch that might have taken minutes or even hours using Hadoop or Spark for the right kinds of queries. And if you're doing something relatively straightforward, like an average or an aggregate histograms or something like that, you can also nest aggregations together and this is where the real power comes in. This allows you to do more complex analysis on your data and to pair your aggregations with search queries to narrow down the things that you're trying to analyze.
