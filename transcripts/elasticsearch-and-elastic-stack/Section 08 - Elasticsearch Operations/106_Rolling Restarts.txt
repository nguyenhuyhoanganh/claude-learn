# Rolling Restarts

Let's discuss rolling restarts. Sometimes you need to perform maintenance on your cluster. You need to upgrade the operating systems, apply software updates, supply OS updates, upgrade your version of Elasticsearch or locked sash or Cabana or whatever. And to make this go as quickly and efficiently as possible, we need to disable index reallocation while you're doing this sort of a thing. Now, if you remember, whenever you bring down a node in your cluster, Elasticsearch will try to recover from that automatically and start moving shards all over the place. In response to that, you don't really want that going on when you're just trying to do a rolling restart across your cluster to apply some software upgrade. So the trick is you need to disable that rebalancing while you're operating on a given node. So here's the procedure you're going to follow for doing a rolling restart across your cluster for whatever maintenance you have to do on it. First, you've got to stop indexing new data if you can. Things are going to go a lot easier and have less possibility of bad things happening. If you can stop indexing new data, so shut down the rights to your Elasticsearch cluster, then you're going to disable shard allocation on the cluster, meaning that when it sees a node go down, it's not going to go. Something failed. I need to redistribute everything. Instead, you're going to disable that. Then you go ahead and shut down the node that you're operating on, perform your maintenance on it, and restart it if you need to, and then confirm that it rejoins the cluster. At that point, you can re-enable chart allocation to put things back to normal. Just wait for the cluster to turn to green status. And once that's done, just keep repeating those steps for each other node in your cluster as you go through them one at a time to apply whatever updates you need to apply. At that point, you can resume indexing new data because you no longer have an inconsistent state throughout your cluster. So that is the procedure to follow. Ideally, if you're going to do some sort of a rolling maintenance across your cluster as a whole, if you're an administrator of an Elasticsearch cluster, this is something you're going to have to get good at. So here's a little bit of a cheat sheet for getting through the process. To first disable chart allocation, you're going to use the underscore cluster slash settings and set persistent to cluster dot routing allocation dot enable to primaries and that will disable allocation across the cluster. At that point, you might want to issue a flush command as well just to speed things up. Once that's done, you can go ahead and shut down Elasticsearch across the whole cluster using pseudo bin system controls, stop Elasticsearch service, do whatever maintenance you need to do and then restart your cluster. Wait for the masters to come up, wait for it to get back into yellow status, and then you can re-enable chart allocation by setting that same setting to null.
