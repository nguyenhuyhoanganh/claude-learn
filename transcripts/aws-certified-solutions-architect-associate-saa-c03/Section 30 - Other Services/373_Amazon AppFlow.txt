# Amazon AppFlow

So, now let's talk about Amazon AppFlow. So, AppFlow is a fully managed integration service that enables you to transfer data between Software-as-a-Service applications and AWS. So, these integrations are quite difficult to write, and with AppFlow they're very easy. So, sources of data could be for example, Salesforce, SAP, Zendesk, Slack, ServiceNow and Salesforce is something that can come up in the exam. And the destinations you can send this data to can be Amazon S3, Amazon Redshift or even non-AWS targets such as SnowFlake and Salesforce. You can define the integration to run on a schedule or in response to specific events or on demand. You get, from within AppFlow, data transformation capabilities such as filtering and validation. And the data is encrypted over the public internet or you can transfer it privately using PrivateLink. The idea is that with AppFlow, you don't spend some time writing the integration, you just leverage these APIs immediately and you enjoy the data within your own accounts. So, in the AppFlow interface, you will have a source and you can select many different sources, but some of the important ones you need to remember are the ones I said from before, including Salesforce. And then using AppFlow, you can send this data into various destinations as you can see, Redshift, S3, and so on. So, that's it for AppFlow, just an introduction. I hope you liked it.
