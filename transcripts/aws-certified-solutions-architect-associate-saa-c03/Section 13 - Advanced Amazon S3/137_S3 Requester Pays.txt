# S3 Requester Pays

So here is a feature that you may get tested on at the exam. And this is called S3 Requester Pays. And the name is quite obvious. So, in general, from what we've seen, the bucket owners will pay for all of the Amazon S3 storage and also the data-transfer costs associated with their buckets. So let's take an example. We have a set of buckets, we're storing some objects in it, and then a requester, so, a user will download a file from our bucket. And then what happens is that the networking cost is also being billed to the owner of the bucket and the objects, okay? But, in case you are having a lot of heavy files and some of the customers want to download them, maybe you want to enable Requester Pays buckets. And in that case, the requester will pay instead of the bucket owner for the data download of the objects. So let's take an example again. The owner still will pay for the storage costs of the objects within the bucket, but now the requester will download the object and the requester will be the one paying for the networking costs associated with that download. The idea is that this is very helpful if you want to start sharing large data sets with other accounts. And for it to do so, the requester must not be anonymous, okay? The requester must be authenticated in AWS because when it is authenticated with AWS, then AWS knows to bill the requester of that request for that specific download of the object, okay? So that's it, just a feature to remember. It can come up in the exam in a scenario. I hope you liked it, and I will see you in the next lecture.
