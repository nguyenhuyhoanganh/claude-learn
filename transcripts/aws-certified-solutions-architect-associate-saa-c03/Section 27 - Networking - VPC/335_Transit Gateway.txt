# Transit Gateway

Okay, so if we look at common network topologies in AWS it can become quite complicated. For example, you have many VPC and you want to peer them together, then you want to establish some VPN connections and direct connect, and then you have a direct connect gateway to connect to multiple VPC at a time, and this can become very, very complicated in terms of network topology. So, AWS came up with the transit gateway to solve that problem, and you're going to have a transitive peering connection between thousands of VPC, your on-premises data center, your site-to-site VPN, direct connects in a hub-and-spoke star connection. So let's have a diagram. We have transit gateway in the center and you can connect multiple VPCs through the transit gateway. So in this example, we don't need to peer the VPCs together, they are connected transitively through the transit gateway. So in this example, all the VPCs can talk to each other. But also, you can connect a direct connect gateway to the transit gateway, so it shows you have a direct connect connection directly in to many different VPC. Or if you are preferring site-to-site VPN and VPN connections, you can connect your customer gateway and your VPN connection into your transit gateway. Again, one more time, giving you access to all these VPC as part of the transit gateway. So this really solves some network problems. It's a regional resource and it can work cross-region, and you can share your transit gateway across accounts by using the resource access manager. You can also peer transit gateways across region. So, how do you define who can talk to what? Well, you need to create route tables for your transit gateway to limit which VPC can talk to another, which connection have access to each other and so on. So, you get full control over the routing of all the traffic within the transit gateway to give you network security. So, as I said, it works with direct connect gateway and VPN connections, and it is the only service in AWS that supports IP multicast, so if you see IP multicast at the exam, just know that it is transit gateway you have to use. So, another use case for transit gateway is to increase the bandwidth of your site-to-site VPN connection using ECMP. So, it's quite technical but the question can come up in the exam. So, ECMP means equal-cost multi-path routing. It's a routing strategy to allow to forward a packet over multiple best path. And the use case, as I said, is to create multiple site-to-site VPN connections to increase the bandwidth of your connection to AWS using a site-to-site VPN. So, let's take this example where we have a transit gateway and we have four VPCs attached to our transit gateway, and we have a corporate data center that is connected using site-to-site VPN to a transit gateway. So when you establish a site-to-site VPN connection there are actually two tunnels, one going forward and one going back. When you are connecting such a VPN into a VPC directly, both of these tunnels are used as part of one connections, but when using the transit gateway two tunnels can be used at a time, so this is why you see two lines in this diagram. But with transit gateway, you can have multiple site-to-site VPN so you can create a second site-to-site VPN attachment and into your transit gateway, so this creates four tunnels. And so, when you have four tunnels of site-to-site VPN, you are increasing the throughput of your connection which is something you cannot do if you were to connect your corporate data center directly into a VPC. So, if you do a VPN to a virtual private gateway, you get one tunnel, in fact one connection into one VPC, and this connection gives you 1.25 Gbps as the maximum throughput, and you are limited. And in this case, a VPN connection is made of two tunnels. But if you are using a VPN into a transit gateway, you get one site-to-site VPN into many VPC because they're all connected transitively to the same transit gateway. And also, one site-to-site VPN connection gives you 2.5 Gbps thanks to ECMP, because the two tunnels are going to be used through that strategy. But also, you can add more site-to-site VPN connections into transit gateway, for example two or three, to double or triple your throughputs through ECMP. So this is an exam question you need to know. Obviously when you do the setup, you're going to have to pay for each GB of data going through the transit gateway, so there's an added cost to this performance optimization.
